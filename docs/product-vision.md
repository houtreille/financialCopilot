# Product Vision

## Vision

Financial Copilot is a personal financial digital twin and scenario laboratory.

It consolidates financial data and turns it into understandable projections, probabilities and decision-support information.

Its main purpose is to help answer:

> Am I financially on track to regain control of my time?

## Personal context

The project starts from a practical need.

Financial information is currently distributed across:

* spreadsheets;
* investment simulations;
* loan amortisation schedules;
* pension estimates;
* asset valuations;
* one-off calculations;
* financial discussions and notes.

The application should progressively become the single source of truth for this information.

## Product objective

The product should monitor:

* current net worth;
* FIRE-eligible capital;
* liquid and illiquid assets;
* liabilities;
* monthly savings capacity;
* investment contributions;
* expected pension income;
* current and future expenses;
* progress towards financial independence.

It should support decisions such as:

* choosing a realistic retirement age;
* evaluating part-time work;
* comparing investment contributions;
* evaluating the impact of a large purchase;
* deciding whether an asset sale is required;
* measuring the impact of a market crash;
* understanding changes in the projected FIRE date.

## Development objective

The project must also restore motivation and enjoyment in software development.

This is not a secondary benefit. It is a core project requirement.

The project should prioritise work that creates curiosity and satisfaction, including:

* Monte-Carlo methods;
* probability distributions;
* financial modelling;
* optimisation;
* scenario generation;
* data visualisation;
* AI-assisted interpretation;
* experimentation with language and framework features.

The project should remain clean and maintainable without recreating the constraints of a large corporate system.

## Product principles

### Source of truth before automation

The data model and calculation engines must be reliable before automatic integrations are introduced.

### Calculation engine before AI assistant

The AI assistant must call tested calculation tools. It must not invent financial results.

### Scenarios before predictions

The application should show a range of possible futures rather than claim to know one exact future.

### Decisions before dashboards

Charts and metrics should help answer a decision-relevant question.

### Private by design

Personal financial data must remain outside the public repository and must not be sent to external systems accidentally.

### Personal use before SaaS

The initial product is designed for one household. Multi-user and commercial requirements are out of scope.

## Success criteria

The product succeeds when:

1. it improves understanding of the household's financial trajectory;
2. it makes the owner want to develop the next feature.

Both criteria should influence the roadmap.
